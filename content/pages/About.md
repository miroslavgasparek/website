I am a second-year doctoral student of Engineering Science at the University of Oxford under the supervision of [Professor Antonis Papachristodoulou](http://sysos.eng.ox.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/User:Antonis) and [Professor Harrison Steel](https://steel.ac/people/). I graduated from Imperial College London with MEng in Biomedical Engineering in 2019. My research interests lie at the intersection of **synthetic biology**, **control engineering**, **machine learning** and **modelling of biological systems**.

Why do I care about synthetic biology? I am convinced that the applications of the engineering methods and utilization of the corresponding, output-oriented mindset is the best way to *address* the major healthcare, economic, and environmental challenges of our times. And I think that engineering biology is also the best way to *learn* about living systems and to understand ourselves better. Because, as Richard Feynman expressed it nicely, *"What I cannot create, I do not understand."*

The specific engineering problems that I am currently working on are:

* **Design and analysis of complex gene circuits**
* **Optimal control of biological systems using classical control theory approaches** 
* **Data-driven and machine learning-based control of biological systems and beyond**

To learn more about some  of my projects, you can read my [**Blog**](https://www.miroslavgasparek.com/category/blog.html), where I share my thoughts, summarize what I learned from my experiences, and present small selection of my (mostly programming-related) projects. <br>
